For this step, you will need the motor support, the electronic card, 4 screws and a screwdriver.

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/5-1024x683.png)

Slide the motor cable into the opening on the electronic card. They will slide in easily.

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/6-1-1024x683.png)

Insert then screw the first 4 screws from the upper face of the electronic card. There are 2 per side.

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/7-1-1024x683.png)

> Be careful to connect the right motor to the right connector and the left to the left. If the cables are reversed, Eliobot will then move backwards instead of forwards and turn in the opposite direction.

### Step 6: Installing the Bumpers

To protect your new companion and ensure that it functions properly for as long as possible, it comes with two bumpers, a large one at the front and a smaller one at the back.

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/17-1024x683.png)

To assemble them, simply place a bumper on top of the robot at the edge of the card.

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/18-1024x683.png)

Then screw it from below with 2 screws. We then start again with the second.

Insert the sensor pins into the connector underneath Eliobot, with the rounded part of the sensor facing forward.

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/20-1024x683.png)

## Tadam! Here is Eliobot

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/25-1024x683.png)

Be careful to hold Eliobot the right way!
The front is the wider part and the rear is the narrower part.

To turn it on, there is a switch on the rear left edge of the board (right next to the USB socket).

![Alt â€‹â€‹text](/img/tutorial/assembly/23.png)

To charge the battery, it's like with a phone. Simply connect the Eliobot USB cable and you will see a small LED light up next to the socket. When it turns off it means it has finished charging!
